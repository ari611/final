THIS IS JUST THE BEGINNING

Gershon, a scholar in anthropology, explains in her book that branding is 'largely a boondoggle advanced by inspirational speakers and job trainers.' However, it doesn’t assist people in getting jobs. Instead, it only makes us more accepting of an increasingly dehumanized job market that treats workers as products rather than people.
Ordinary people are now told to acquire what once only companies and celebrities required: online “findability,” thousands of Google hits and Twitter followers, a niche of their own, a virtual network of patrons, a personal Wikipedia page and dot-com domain.
Everyone is forced to become a marketer because of the Internet. This leads the job market in putting more emphasis on a person's (product) reputation over talent.
“Here, success is not determined by individuals’ internal sets of skills, motivations, and interests but, rather, by how effectively they are arranged, crystallized, and labeled.”
Personal branding has gained importance for the past decades BUT job stability has decreased.
Union memberships have declined, and the average workweek for full-time adults has extended to 47 hours, an increase of 1.5 hours from a decade ago. Today's millennials are experiencing more job changes, averaging four jobs within their first decade after college, which is double the number compared to the previous generation. Modern workplaces are increasingly demanding more time from employees while providing less job security and fewer protections.
